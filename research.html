Research

Scientific themes and ongoing projects

Research in the Kaur Lab focuses on understanding biological energy conversion and molecular mechanisms using computational biophysics, molecular dynamics simulations, and electronic structure methods.

Photosynthetic Systems

Computational investigation of Photosystem I and II, including pigment–protein interactions, excitation energy transfer, and redox chemistry.

Interfacial Water

Structure and dynamics of hydration water near proteins and cofactors, and its influence on stability, function, and energetics.

Molecular Dynamics Simulations

Classical molecular dynamics simulations using AMBER to study conformational dynamics and biomolecular interactions.

QM/MM & Electronic Structure

Hybrid QM/MM approaches to study electronic properties, charge transfer, and reaction mechanisms in complex systems.

Multiscale Modeling

Integrating atomistic and coarse-grained methods to explore biological processes across length and time scales.
