Research

Computational approaches to understand biological systems at molecular resolution

Research Areas

The Kaur Computational Lab uses molecular simulations, quantum chemistry, and multiscale computational approaches to investigate biological mechanisms at the atomic level.

Computational Biophysics
We investigate structure, dynamics, and molecular interactions in biological systems using computational approaches to reveal the physical principles governing biomolecular function.
Molecular Modeling Biomolecular Dynamics Simulation
Molecular Dynamics Simulations
Classical molecular dynamics simulations are employed to study protein flexibility, conformational changes, hydration behaviour, and molecular interactions across biologically relevant time scales.
AMBER GROMACS Protein Dynamics
QM/MM and Electronic Structure
Hybrid quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics methods are used to explore electronic properties, charge transfer mechanisms, redox reactions, and catalytic processes in complex biomolecules.
QM/MM Quantum Chemistry Electronic Structure
Photosynthetic Systems
Computational studies of photosynthetic complexes including Photosystem I and II, focusing on pigment–protein interactions, energy transfer pathways, and electron transfer processes.
Photosystem I Iron–Sulfur Clusters Energy Conversion
Multiscale Modeling
Combining atomistic simulations and higher-scale approaches to connect molecular interactions with large biological processes.
Multiscale Simulation Coarse Graining
