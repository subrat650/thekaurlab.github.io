Research

Research in the Kaur Lab focuses on understanding biological energy conversion processes using computational biophysics and molecular simulations. Our work aims to uncover the molecular mechanisms governing photosynthesis, hydration dynamics, and protein–environment interactions.

Photosynthetic Systems

We investigate Photosystem I and II using atomistic molecular dynamics and quantum mechanical approaches to understand energy transfer, charge separation, and pigment–protein interactions.

Interfacial Water & Hydration

Our research explores the structure and dynamics of interfacial water near biomolecular surfaces and its role in stability, function, and energy landscapes.

Multiscale Modeling

We employ multiscale computational techniques combining classical MD, QM/MM, and enhanced sampling methods to study complex biological systems.
